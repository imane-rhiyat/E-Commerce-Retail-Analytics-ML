## 1. Background & Business Problem

### 🔹 Situation
An international online retail company generates thousands of customer transactions but lacks a structured approach to understanding customer purchasing behavior. While sales data is readily available, it provides limited insight into which customers generate the greatest value, which customers are becoming inactive, and where marketing efforts should be focused.

### 🔹 Business Problem
Without customer segmentation, all customers are treated similarly despite significant differences in purchasing frequency, spending behavior, and recency of activity. This creates several distinct business challenges:
* **Inefficient Marketing:** Promotional campaigns target low-value and inactive customers blindly, diluting ad spend.
* **Missed Revenue:** The business misses critical opportunities to proactively retain and reward high-value accounts.
* **Low Visibility:** Limited insights into overall customer lifetime value and long-term purchasing patterns.
* **Stagnant Strategy:** Difficulty prioritizing customer retention strategies based on concrete data.

### 🔹 Project Objective
This project develops an end-to-end customer analytics pipeline that transforms more than 500,000 e-commerce transactions into actionable business intelligence. 

By utilizing **Python** for data preparation, **MySQL** for analytical modeling, **SQL** for business analysis, **RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) Feature Engineering**, **K-Means Clustering** for customer segmentation, and **Power BI** for interactive visualization, this pipeline enables hyper-targeted, data-driven marketing and retention decisions.

### 🔹 Business Goal
Develop a data-driven customer segmentation framework that enables targeted marketing, improves customer retention efforts, and supports strategic business decision-making.
